\begin{abstract}
The flavor sector of the Standard Model is currently parameterized by the arbitrary mixing angles of the CKM and PMNS matrices, offering no reason for the disparate mixing behaviors of quarks and neutrinos. In this work, we posit that flavor mixing is a Boundary State Transition governed by the geometric alignment of the 5-fold lattice symmetry with the 4-dimensional manifold. We derive the Gatto-Sartori-Tonin (GST) Relation from first principles, proving that the Weak Mixing Angle ($\sin^2 \theta_W$) and the Cabibbo Angle ($\sin \theta_C$) are structurally coupled partitions of the systemic channel capacity. We demonstrate that the disparity between quark and neutrino mixing arises from their structural duality: quarks interact via Interface Drag (small angles), while neutrinos interact via Global Symmetry (large angles). The resulting model calculates the Cabibbo angle ($\theta_C \approx 13.0^{\circ}$), the Jarlskog Invariant ($J \approx 3.08 \times 10^{-5}$), and the Solar Neutrino angle to high precision without free parameters, identifying CP Violation as the inescapable topological phase offset of the Chiral Diode.
\end{abstract}
