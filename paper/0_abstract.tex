\begin{abstract}
The flavor sector of the Standard Model is currently parameterized by the arbitrary mixing angles of the CKM and PMNS matrices, offering no reason for the disparate mixing behaviors of quarks and neutrinos. In this work, we posit that flavor mixing is a \textbf{Boundary State Transition} governed by the geometric alignment of the 5-fold lattice symmetry with the 4-dimensional manifold. We derive the Gatto-Sartori-Tonin (GST) Relation from first principles. We demonstrate that the disparity between quark and neutrino mixing arises from their structural duality: quarks interact via Interface Drag (small angles), while neutrinos interact via Global Symmetry (large angles). The resulting model derives the Cabibbo angle ($\theta_C \approx \CabibboSineVal$), the Jarlskog Invariant ($J \approx \JarlskogMatrixVal$), and the Solar Neutrino angle ($\sin^2 \theta_{12} \approx \SolarAngleVal$) to high precision without free parameters. A zero-degree-of-freedom global fit of the flavor sector yields a total $\chi^2 \approx \flavorTotalChiVal$, identifying CP Violation not as an arbitrary parameter, but as the inescapable topological phase offset of the Chiral Diode ($\delta_{CP} \approx \CPPhaseVal$).
\end{abstract}
